About Chi-Jui

I am a Human-Computer Interaction researcher. My research interests include sensing technologies, mobile devices, ubiquitous computing, proxemic interaction, and machine learning. I build novel supporting toolkits for group collaborations and multi-device interactions, and leverage machine intelligence to enhance existing sensing capabilities within interactive spaces. Recently, I have developed a new tracking infrastructure that enables HCI researchers to build and evaluate proxemic-aware, activity and device-centric interfaces. This work has been accepted to a top-tier conference (CHI).

Currently, I am working as an English teaching assisstant at Wanrong Junior High School, Hualien, Taiwan for substitute military service, expected to complete my service in Januaray, 2018. I have a Master of Research in Computational Statistics and Machine Learning from University College London and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from University of St Andrews. During my studies, I worked closely with Professor Nicolai Marquardt and Professor Steven Houben at UCL Interaction Centre and Professor David Harris-Birtill at St Andrews HCI Research Group.

EagleSense is a system infrastructure for tracking people and devices in interactive spaces using real-time top-view depth-sensing. It enables HCI researchers to build new proxemic and multi-device interactions. Our results show that the system can recognize six different human postures and activities with 90% accuracy at 30 FPS.

Out of Sight is a toolkit that extends the Kinect v2 SDK for tracking occluded human joint positions. It combines the skeleton stream of multiple Kinects using a coordinate transformation algorithm to track the position of people during occlusion. It enables applications to "see through" obstructions. The tracking accuracy is within 16cm.

Recomputation is the process of creating a working environment for a piece of computer program, so that it can be referenced, maintained, and deployed in the future. Recompute is a web application which automates the process of recomputation. It takes a GitHub repository URL as input and creates a virtual machine, within which the computer program is rebuilt from source. Anyone can download the virtual machine and run the program from anywhere.

Group Collaboration

The project studies whether depth-sensing data visualization can amplify user's ability to detect visual notifications and increase their awareness of other surrounding users. It measures user's speed and accuracy at solving a task that requires the awareness of other users' spatial location.
